# Bayes' theorem

Bayes' theorem updates belief after observing evidence. In ML, this appears in naive Bayes classifiers, diagnostic reasoning, and posterior inference.

\[P(A|B)=\frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}\]

The denominator is a normalization term. It ensures the posterior is a valid probability after accounting for every way the evidence could occur.
